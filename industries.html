Industry Focus

We focus on sectors where buyers consistently deploy capital: recurring revenue, essential services, compliance-aware operations, and fragmented markets where consolidation creates value. Below are core categories we often serve—and the buyer logic behind them.

Why These Industries Are Being Acquired

Recurring & Repeatable Revenue

Buyers pay premiums for revenue they can forecast: contracts, subscriptions, repeat purchasing, and embedded customer relationships.

Lower Diligence Risk

Strong SOPs, compliance posture, clear reporting, and reduced concentration minimize retrade leverage and accelerate closing.

Platform + Add-On Potential

Scalable businesses enable expansion: new geographies, new services, tuck-ins, cross-sell, and operational leverage.

Core Industry Categories

Home & Commercial Services

HVAC, plumbing, electrical, restoration, pest, roofing, and essential services

Non-discretionary demand Local brand moat Route density Maintenance plans
Why it’s being acquired
  • Essential spend: repairs and replacements happen regardless of cycles.
  • Scale efficiencies: dispatch, pricing, marketing, purchasing, and training centralize well.
  • Repeat revenue: service plans stabilize cash flow and improve valuation.
  • Fragmentation: platform + tuck-in strategies create outsized value.
What buyers look for
  • call booking rate, average ticket, margin by service line.
  • technician productivity, reviews, lead source mix.
  • service agreement penetration and renewal discipline.

B2B Services

Recurring services with long-term customer relationships and predictable demand

High retention Contracted revenue Low churn Cross-sell
Why it’s being acquired
  • Stickiness: embedded workflows create switching costs.
  • Predictability: contracts and repeat behavior improve underwriting confidence.
  • Scalable growth: repeatable go-to-market and add-ons expand wallet share.
  • Operational leverage: standardization can lift margins at scale.
What buyers look for
  • renewals, contract terms, pricing discipline.
  • pipeline reporting, CAC logic, upsell motion.
  • key-person risk and leadership bench depth.

Specialty & Compliance-Aware Services

Services tied to compliance, reporting, risk management, or regulated operations

Repeat demand Process maturity High trust Lower churn
Why it’s being acquired
  • Durable demand: compliance and risk don’t disappear in downturns.
  • Trust relationships: referrals and repeat engagements support growth.
  • Cleaner diligence: documentation and controls reduce friction and delays.
  • Cross-sell: buyers add new services across the same client base.
What buyers look for
  • client concentration, documented controls, process ownership.
  • repeat engagement patterns and pricing ladder clarity.
  • team utilization, workflow capacity planning.

Healthcare & Health Services

Service lines with stable demand, operating repeatability, and scalable expansion

Stable demand Process-driven Capacity expansion Compliance
Why it’s being acquired
  • Structural demand: ongoing needs support long-term growth.
  • Platform expansion: multi-location rollouts and adjacent service line growth.
  • Operational improvements: systems + staffing optimization can lift margins.
  • Quality + controls: mature operations reduce buyer risk.
What buyers look for
  • compliance posture, staffing stability, clean reporting.
  • unit economics, scheduling efficiency, utilization.
  • documentation quality and repeatable workflows.

Tech-Enabled Services

Operational services improved by software, automation, analytics, or integrations

Scalable delivery Data visibility Process leverage Upsell
Why it’s being acquired
  • Margin expansion: automation reduces cost-to-serve as the base grows.
  • Operational moat: integrations and workflows increase stickiness.
  • Productized delivery: repeatable service packages scale faster.
  • Faster underwriting: good reporting improves buyer confidence.
What buyers look for
  • onboarding time, churn/retention, and expansion revenue.
  • unit economics, pricing discipline, delivery repeatability.
  • security posture (if relevant).

Industrial & Field Services

Inspection, maintenance, repair, compliance-driven fieldwork and uptime services

Mission-critical Compliance demand Repeat customers Specialized labor
Why it’s being acquired
  • Critical service need: customers prioritize uptime, safety, and compliance.
  • Predictable cycles: recurring inspections and maintenance intervals.
  • Scale + coverage: buyers expand geography and service capability.
  • Pricing power: expertise + reliability support premium pricing.
What buyers look for
  • job profitability tracking and standardized quoting.
  • workforce utilization, safety posture, SOP maturity.
  • customer concentration and contract quality.
