Industry Focus

We work with owner-led businesses in industries that buyers consistently seek: recurring or repeatable revenue, mission-critical services, compliance-aware operations, and durable demand. Below are a few of the categories we focus on most often—and why they are actively being acquired.

Why These Industries Are Being Acquired

Recurring & Repeatable Revenue

Buyers pay premiums for revenue they can forecast—contracts, subscriptions, repeat purchasing behavior, and embedded customer relationships.

Risk-Managed Operations

Mature SOPs, compliance discipline, strong reporting, and low customer concentration reduce diligence friction and increase certainty.

Platform + Add-On Potential

Acquirers like businesses they can scale: multiple locations, new services, cross-sell, tuck-ins, or expansion into adjacent markets.

Core Industry Categories

Home Services

HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing, pest, and other essential services

Non-discretionary demand Local brand moat Route density Maintenance plans
Why it’s being acquired
  • Essential spend: repairs and replacements happen regardless of cycles.
  • Scale efficiencies: centralized dispatch, pricing, marketing, and purchasing.
  • Repeat + membership revenue: service plans stabilize cash flow and improve multiples.
  • Fragmented markets: platform + tuck-in strategies create outsized value.
What buyers look for
  • call booking rate, average ticket, gross margin by job type.
  • technician productivity, customer concentration, inbound lead mix, reviews.
  • service agreement penetration and renewal discipline.

B2B Services

Recurring services with long-term customer relationships

High retention Contracted revenue Low churn Cross-sell
Why it’s being acquired
  • Stickiness: switching costs and embedded workflows support retention.
  • Predictability: contracted revenue and repeat purchasing improve certainty.
  • Scalable growth: replicable go-to-market and add-on services expand wallet share.
  • Operational leverage: standardization improves margins as scale increases.
What buyers look for
  • renewal rates, contract terms, pricing discipline, service-level delivery.
  • sales pipeline reporting, CAC payback logic, upsell/cross-sell motion.
  • key-person risk and depth of leadership bench.

Financial & Specialized Services

Compliance-aware services with repeat client demand

Recurring clients Regulated discipline Process maturity High trust
Why it’s being acquired
  • Durable demand: services tied to compliance, reporting, or risk management.
  • High trust relationships: repeat engagement and referrals support growth.
  • Operational systems: documentation and controls reduce diligence friction.
  • Cross-sell potential: buyers expand services across the same client base.
What buyers look for
  • client concentration, compliance posture, and documented processes.
  • revenue segmentation and repeat engagement patterns.
  • team utilization and workflow capacity planning.

Healthcare & Health Services

Service lines with stable demand and operational repeatability

Stable demand Process-driven Capacity expansion Compliance
Why it’s being acquired
  • Structural demand: ongoing needs support long-term growth.
  • Platform expansion: multi-location rollouts and adjacent service line growth.
  • Operational improvements: systems and staffing optimization lift margins.
  • Quality + compliance: mature operations reduce buyer risk.
What buyers look for
  • payor mix clarity (where applicable), compliance controls, and staffing stability.
  • unit economics (per visit / per procedure), scheduling efficiency, and capacity utilization.
  • documentation quality and clean metrics reporting.

Tech-Enabled Services

Operational services improved by software, automation, and analytics

Scalable delivery Data visibility Process leverage Upsell
Why it’s being acquired
  • Margin expansion: automation improves cost-to-serve as the base grows.
  • Operational moat: integrations and workflows increase stickiness.
  • Productized delivery: repeatable service packages scale faster.
  • Analytics: strong reporting supports better underwriting and faster deals.
What buyers look for
  • repeatable delivery processes, onboarding time, and churn/retention.
  • unit economics, pricing discipline, and expansion revenue.
  • security/compliance posture (where relevant).

Industrial & Field Services

Inspection, maintenance, repair, compliance-driven fieldwork

Mission-critical Compliance demand Repeat customers Specialized labor
Why it’s being acquired
  • Critical service need: customers prioritize uptime, safety, and compliance.
  • Predictable cycles: recurring inspections and maintenance intervals.
  • Scale and coverage: buyers expand geography and service capability.
  • Pricing power: expertise + reliability supports premium pricing.
What buyers look for
  • job profitability tracking and standardized quoting.
  • workforce utilization, safety records, and SOP maturity.
  • customer concentration and contract terms.
