Repeat & Essential Services

HVAC, Plumbing & Electrical

Why buyers want it: non-discretionary demand, recurring service calls, membership plans, and strong local brand economics. Fragmentation creates roll-up opportunity and purchasing leverage.

What increases valuation: service mix > install mix, tech utilization, call center discipline, and add-on acquisition playbook.

Recurring demandFragmented marketAdd-on M&A ready

Restoration & Emergency Services

Why buyers want it: time-sensitive work, insurance-driven revenue, and repeat referrals from adjusters and property managers. Buyers like the defensibility of response infrastructure.

What increases valuation: referral diversity, cycle-time KPI rigor, and documented compliance/safety standards.

Insurance tailwindHigh urgencyOperational moat

Facilities Maintenance (Interior/Exterior)

Why buyers want it: contract-based recurring revenue and “mission-critical” uptime support for multi-site operators (retail, industrial, healthcare).

What increases valuation: contract structure, renewal rates, dispatch efficiency, and regional density.

Contracted revenueRoute densityMulti-location

Commercial Landscaping

Why buyers want it: sticky maintenance contracts and the ability to add higher-margin enhancements. Consolidation increases route density and labor utilization.

What increases valuation: customer retention, branch-level P&Ls, and dependable labor strategy.

Recurring contractsDensity economicsUpsell potential

Roofing & Exterior Residential Services

Why buyers want it: durable replacement cycle, storm-driven demand spikes, and repeatable sales/production systems.

What increases valuation: compliance, claims discipline, lead-gen diversification, and production controls that reduce rework.

Replacement cycleSystematizable opsScalable sales

Access & Security Systems

Why buyers want it: recurring monitoring/service, compliance-driven demand, and high switching costs for commercial clients.

What increases valuation: service contract base, response SLAs, and technician utilization.

Switching costsService revenueCompliance

What increases valuation: retention/renewals, standardized reporting, and diversified portfolio mix.

Where We Focus

Services

Home Services

HVAC, plumbing, electrical, restoration, pest control, roofing, and related categories with consistent local demand. Buyers are attracted to fragmentation, route density, and the ability to standardize operations.

  • Why acquired: consolidation + repeatability + cross-sell potential
  • Key drivers: membership plans, attach rates, utilization, reviews
  • Diligence focus: job costing, warranty exposure, dispatch discipline
Contracts

B2B & Facility Services

Commercial cleaning, security, fire/life safety, specialty maintenance, and compliance-heavy services. Attractive when contracts are sticky and delivery is standardized.

  • Why acquired: predictable revenue + multi-location expansion
  • Key drivers: renewal rates, SLA performance, contract length
  • Diligence focus: labor mix, churn, service-level penalties
Field Ops

Industrial & Field Services

Maintenance, repair, testing/inspection, and specialized field operations. Buyers favor strong safety culture, utilization reporting, and durable customer relationships.

  • Why acquired: mission-critical demand + geographic expansion
  • Key drivers: backlog visibility, utilization, safety record
  • Diligence focus: insurance history, key-person dependency
Recurring Need

Healthcare Services

Service models supported by demographic demand and recurring utilization where compliance and documentation are strong.

  • Why acquired: demand tailwinds + platform/add-on expansion
  • Key drivers: retention, referrals, multi-site playbook
  • Diligence focus: compliance, credentialing, payer mix
Efficiency

Tech-Enabled Services

Services businesses that leverage software, data, automation, or workflow tools to deliver outcomes more efficiently—especially with recurring contracts.

  • Why acquired: margin expansion + repeatable delivery model
  • Key drivers: retention, ARPA growth, automation leverage
  • Diligence focus: churn drivers, security posture, rev rec
Standardization

Professional Services

Specialized professional service lines where delivery can be standardized and customer concentration is controlled.

  • Why acquired: recurring revenue + roll-up potential
  • Key drivers: delivery playbooks, leadership depth
  • Diligence focus: founder dependence, pipeline quality
